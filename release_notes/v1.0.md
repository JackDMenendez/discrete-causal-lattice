## Why v1.0

v0.98-RC was a beta release for review with explicit open work.  v1.0
closes the release-candidate cycle: three substantive proof-sketch
items that v0.98-RC marked open are now in the paper at the
sufficiency-with-pointers level, three new audit-table rows reflect
new experimental work and corrected accounting of the SM-derivation
claim, and the title-page metadata + page layout were polished
through a paragraph-by-paragraph audit pass.

The framework's verified scope at v1.0:

- The Dirac equation, the Born rule (now with Gleason uniqueness),
  hydrogen quantization at four significant figures, gravity as
  clock-density refraction (now with an explicit weak-field metric
  tensor), and the structural form of the induced gauge action.
- $\mathrm{Aut}(\Tdiamond, \mathcal{A}=1) \supseteq SO(3,1) \times
  U(1)$ (dim 7) on the existing per-site $\mathbb{C}^2$ amplitude,
  proven by computational verification.
- The full Standard Model gauge-group conjecture — $SO(3,1) \times
  SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$, dim 18 — is stated precisely as a
  Lie-algebra equality and identified as requiring per-site
  $\mathbb{C}^2$ isospin and $\mathbb{C}^3$ colour structural
  extensions; this is the central open problem and the subject of a
  companion paper.

## Summary of changes since v0.98-RC

### New experimental work

1. **`exp_12c` (long-horizon escape diagnosis).**  Grid sweep across
   $\{65, 81, 97, 113\}^3$ resolves the v0.98-RC question about
   long-horizon two-body stability.  Finding: orbit escapes at tick
   ~140 on $\geq 81^3$ and tick ~2000 on $65^3$.  The $65^3$
   "stability" was a finite-volume artefact (boundary reflections
   confining the wave packet near $R_1$).  Init-mode and drift-mode
   controls rule out initial-condition asymmetry and operator drift
   as alternative causes.  New audit-table row, `PART`.

2. **`exp_12d_tight` (lock-in resonance grid-independence).**
   Cross-grid validation of `exp_12`'s 4-sig-fig PASS.  Pre-escape
   measurement window (tick 30–119) across grids 65/81/97/113.
   Result: $r$ at $k_\text{Bohr} = 0.0971$ in $[11.54, 12.30]$ across
   all four grids ($\Delta r \approx 0.8$); three of four
   $k_\text{min}$ within $\pm 0.003$ of $k_\text{Bohr}$.  Lock-in
   attractor is grid-independent.  New audit-table row, `PART`.

3. **`exp_12d_outlier_trace` (outlier characterisation).**  The two
   $k = 0.101$ outliers on $81^3$ and $113^3$ from `exp_12d_tight`
   trace to early-escape tail of `exp_12c`'s metastable-orbit
   mechanism (abrupt escape at tick 119), not to a $k = 0.101$
   instability.  Strengthens (rather than weakens) the
   grid-independence claim.

### New theoretical work (proof sketches + paper-section patches)

4. **Tier 1: Born rule via Gleason** (§10.7.1 of the paper, new
   subsubsection).  Addresses the necessity question for $|\psi|^2$
   that the v0.98-RC text only handled with a sufficiency argument
   (path counting).  Gleason's theorem on the framework's emergent
   Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}_\text{session} = \ell^2(\Tdiamond,
   \mathbb{C}^2)$ forces the quadratic form.  Source note:
   `notes/born_rule_gleason_uniqueness.md`.

5. **Tier 2: explicit metric tensor** (§7.7 of the paper, new
   subsection).  $g_{\mu\nu}$ as a functional of $\rho_\text{clock}$
   in the weak-field limit: $g_{\mu\nu} = \mathrm{diag}(-(\rho/\bar
   \rho)^{-2}, (\rho/\bar\rho)^{2}\delta_{ij}) + O(\varphi^2/c^4)$.
   Builds on the existing eq:boltzmann_clock derivation in §7.3,
   provides the closed form that the v0.98-RC version stated would
   follow.  Source note:
   `notes/g_mu_nu_clock_density_identification.md`.

6. **Tier 2: refined automorphism conjecture** (§15 of the paper,
   rewrite).  The conjecture $\mathrm{Aut}(\Tdiamond, \mathcal{A}=1)
   = SO(3,1) \times SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ is now stated
   as a precise 18-dimensional Lie-algebra equality with explicit
   decomposition: $SO(3,1)$ and $U(1)$ are *established* on the
   existing per-site $\mathbb{C}^2$ (dim 7), with computational
   verification that per-site $SU(2)$ overlaps with the Lorentz
   rotation subgroup rather than forming a separate factor;
   $SU(2)_W$ and $SU(3)$ require per-site internal extensions
   ($\mathbb{C}^2$ isospin and $\mathbb{C}^3$ colour) that are
   structural additions.  Six computational scaffolding scripts in
   `src/utilities/` establish each component.  Source note:
   `notes/lie_algebra_automorphism_proof_sketch.md`.

## Reproducibility

All numerical experiments still run on a single consumer-grade
desktop with NumPy on CPU; no GPU acceleration required.  The
$\geq 113^3$ grid runs in `exp_12c` and `exp_12d_tight` complete in
under 2 hours wall-clock on a 4-core machine.  `exp_12c`'s
escape-onset finding is reproducible at `data/exp_12c_grid_*.log`;
`exp_12d_tight`'s grid-independence finding at
`data/exp_12d_tight_grid_*.log`.

A 10-line minimal verification snippet (Appendix C reproducibility
quickstart) reproduces a smoke test of `exp_12b` in ~14 s.
