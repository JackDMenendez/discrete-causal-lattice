## At a glance

This release candidate is the first version of the paper to carry
**sharpened numerical predictions** and **black-hole thermodynamics
derivations** alongside the framework's core analytic results. The
manuscript builds clean at 109 pages, all figures land in their proper
sections, and the audit table has no remaining `STUB` entries.

The framework derives, from a single axiom — conservation of probability,
$\mathcal{A} = 1$, on a bipartite three-dimensional causal lattice
$\mathcal{T}_\diamond^3$ — the following results without further postulates:

- **The Dirac equation** with gamma matrices forced by the RGB/CMY
  bipartite geometry (analytic derivation, six-step continuum-limit
  programme; rotational invariance recovered automatically by the
  frame condition).
- **The Born rule** as a conservation theorem rather than a measurement
  axiom.
- **Atomic quantization as an Arnold-tongue attractor**, recovering the
  hydrogen Bohr radius to four significant figures with no parameter
  tuning (`exp_12` two-body simulation: $k_\mathrm{min} = 0.0970$
  vs.\ $k_\mathrm{Bohr} = 0.0971$).
- **Gravity as clock-density refraction**, with the Einstein field
  equations as the continuum limit and falsifiable short-range
  corrections to the $1/r^2$ law from finite-lattice path counting.
- **Photon emission as a structural necessity** of $\mathcal{A} = 1$
  (numerical implementation in `exp_19c` verifies the joint
  $\mathcal{A} = 1$ + recoil mechanism; event-rate measurements are
  in progress).
- **Black-hole thermodynamics** — both the Bekenstein–Hawking
  area–entropy law $S = k_B A / (4\ell_P^2)$ and the Hawking
  temperature $T_H = \hbar c^3 / (8\pi G M k_B)$ — under stated
  boundary-saturation modelling assumptions.

A discrete bipartite topology with one conservation law turns out to
be sufficient to reproduce, to leading order, the principal results of
quantum mechanics, special relativity, gravitation, and (via the
Arnold-tongue Farey hierarchy) the discrete particle spectrum.

## What is new since v0.9

### Sharpened numerical predictions

Four predictions in §12 (Path Counting and Falsifiable Predictions)
have been promoted from order-of-magnitude estimates to derivations
with explicit uncertainty:

- **P5 — Stark threshold tongue width.** The dimensionless ratio
  $\Delta\omega_\mathrm{tongue} / \omega_e = 0.033 \pm 0.020$ at the
  3:1 resonance is now measured directly from the high-resolution
  harmonic-scan data (`data/exp_harmonic_hires_powermap.npy`) by an
  FWHM-extraction script (`src/utilities/tongue_width_3to1.py`),
  superseding the earlier eyeball estimate of $\sim 0.05$.
- **P5 — Critical Stark field.** A Peierls-substitution derivation
  yields $E_\mathrm{crit}^{(n=1)} \approx 8.5 \times 10^9\,\mathrm{V/m}$
  with a propagated range of $3 \times 10^9$ to $1.4 \times 10^{10}\,\mathrm{V/m}$,
  in principle reachable by trapped-ion DC-field spectroscopy. A
  dimensional inconsistency in the prior formula (a misplaced factor
  of $c$ where $\hbar$ belonged) is corrected. The result is now
  calibration-independent in the continuum limit, replacing a prior
  GRB-suppression claim that is no longer supported.
- **P4 — Muon-lifetime correction.** The $(a^2/6)$ Laplacian of the
  discrete clock-density update gives a specific prefactor under the
  stated assumption that the muon's per-tick rate inherits this
  correction. Predicted shift at Earth's surface:
  $1.3 \times 10^{-48}$ at Compton calibration. Reframed honestly as
  structurally sound but currently unfalsifiable.
- **P6 — Emission-rate clock-density correction.** Updated to
  $\sim 3 \times 10^{-11}$ at Earth's surface using the new
  tongue-width measurement.

### New derivations under stated assumptions

- §7.5 derives the Bekenstein–Hawking entropy from boundary-session
  counting, with the geometric origin of the factor $1/4$ traced to
  $A_\mathrm{session} = 4\ell_P^2$ (RGB/CMY $\times$ R/L spinor
  degrees of freedom) — a stated modelling assumption awaiting a
  Strominger–Vafa-style microstate count.
- §4.6 derives the Hawking temperature via the Unruh–horizon relation
  at the saturation boundary, under the assumption that the boundary
  inherits the Schwarzschild surface gravity. Also corrects a
  pre-existing typo in the Hawking-temperature formula
  ($\hbar c$ → $\hbar c^3$ in numerator).

### Substrate clarification

§2.5 now states explicitly that two natural distances coexist on the
cubic geometry — the cube edge $\ell_P$ and the body-diagonal hop
$h = \sqrt{3}\ell_P$ — and declares the convention that "the lattice
spacing $a$" throughout the paper means the body-diagonal hop distance,
not the cube edge. This clears up a previously ambiguous relation
between the calibration tables and the per-prediction expressions.

## Reproducing the results

All numerical experiments and figure renderers run on a single
consumer-grade workstation using Python and NumPy with no GPU or
distributed-computing requirement. The largest grids
($65^3 \approx 274{,}000$ nodes) complete in seconds to hours per
run. Build the paper with:

```sh
make paper      # 3-pass pdflatex + bibtex
make promote    # copy PDF to stage/
```

Re-run any individual experiment with:

```sh
make -C src/experiments <target>
```

Required: GNU Make $\geq 4.3$ (UCRT64 shell on Windows), MiKTeX or
TeX Live, Python 3 with NumPy/SciPy/Matplotlib.
