# v0.98-RC — Experimental progress, audit-table reconciliation, five-reviewer feedback pass

A release-candidate update building on v0.97-RC's structural
enrichment. Two new experiments (`exp_20` three-arm emission
operator comparison; `exp_12b` long-duration two-body baseline) ran
for the first time, two new audit-table entries were added (both
`PART`, additive), and the paper text was systematically reconciled
with the audit-table statuses with the help of an automated
consistency-check pass introduced this release. Five external
reviewers' feedback was incorporated.

## Why this is `RC` and not v1.0

`exp_20` confirms the operation algebra's **joint-A=1 conservation**
prediction at machine precision under the unitary beam splitter, but
`exp_12b` reveals that the **bare two-body system is itself
unstable** on the 6000-tick horizon at GRID=65³. That's a new open
question (where does the long-horizon two-body instability come
from?) that the paper does not yet answer. The `RC` tag signals a
release for review with explicit open work, not v1.0.

## Headline changes

### exp_20 — three-arm emission operator comparison

| Arm | A_joint_drift | amp_g (final) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| A (control, drain) | 1.0×10⁰ | 1.000 | reproduces `exp_19c` row-5 non-unitarity |
| B (treatment, beam splitter) | **8.9×10⁻¹⁶** | 0.0055 | joint A=1 confirmed at machine precision |
| C (alternative, phase rotation) | 1.0×10⁻¹² | 0.000 | inert as predicted |

Arm B's photon amplitude grew to 0.236 at tick 2019 then *receded*
to 0.005 by tick 4019 — a phase-dependent non-monotonic transfer
not predicted by the pure 2-mode-rotation picture, flagged as a
row-6 caveat for the operation-algebra follow-on (paper #13).

None of the three arms settled the orbit; the cause is upstream of
the emission operator (see `exp_12b`).

### exp_12b — long-duration two-body baseline (no emission)

The bare `exp_12` setup run for 6000 ticks. `r_peak` escapes from R₁
to grid edge (~83) by tick ~2000 and never returns. A=1 preserved
at machine precision throughout. Refines but does not invalidate
`exp_12`'s 4-sig-fig PASS, which scores a different (k-scan
resonance) claim — the scoring distinction is now noted explicitly
in Table 1's caption. The orbital escape seen in all three arms of
`exp_20` is inherited from this baseline, not caused by the
emission operator.

### "One law at three resolutions" — new introduction paragraph

Identifies the clock fluid density
$\rho_\phi(x) = \sum_i \omega_i\,|\psi_i(x)|^2$ as the
$\omega$-weighted form of the joint amplitude density
$\mathcal{A}_\text{joint}(x) = \sum_i |\psi_i(x)|^2$. Probability
conservation, mass-energy conservation, and gravity all share one
structural origin — the framework's "single conservation law"
sharpens from assertion to identification.

### Reviewer-driven framing refinements

Five external reviewers' actionable feedback was incorporated:

- **"No Hilbert space is assumed" softened.** A Hilbert-space
  structure is acknowledged to emerge implicitly from the U(1)
  oscillator and the per-session amplitude constraint rather than
  being postulated a priori.
- **"Standard Model is not a theory — it is an accounting system"
  replaced** with a structural-reinterpretation framing; the
  Lie-algebra question
  Aut(T³_diamond, A=1) =? SO(3,1) × SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1)
  is now explicitly a deferred companion-paper item.
- **"Structurally silent" → "leave open"** in the introduction.
- **PASS / PART / STUB legend** added at first explicit use in the
  introduction; Table 1 caption extended with a scoring-window
  note for `exp_12`.
- **New Appendix C: Reproducibility Quickstart.** Compact 1–2 page
  reviewer-targeted summary with a 10-line minimal verification
  snippet (`EXP12B_TICKS=20` run → reference output `r_peak ≈
  9.877`, `A_drift ≈ 4.4×10⁻¹⁶`). Complements the comprehensive
  Appendix A.
- **Conclusion's *What remains open* subsection extended** with
  three new items: long-horizon two-body stability (`exp_12b`
  finding), numerical $1/g^2$ prefactor for the induced gauge
  action, empirical test of the gauge-sector birefringence
  prediction.
- **Software-engineering vocabulary at the meta-prose layer**
  sanitized to physics-friendly equivalents. Technical sections
  unchanged — their lingo is load-bearing.
- **Front-matter metadata polish**: CC BY 4.0 license statement,
  active ORCID and email links in the author block, Data
  Availability statement, Keywords block.
