# v0.98-RC — Experimental progress + reconciliation: exp_20, exp_12b, two new audit-table rows, paper-text alignment

A beta release building on v0.97-RC's structural enrichment. Two new
experiments (`exp_20` three-arm operator comparison, `exp_12b`
long-duration two-body baseline) ran for the first time, two new
audit-table rows added (both `PART`), and the paper text was
systematically reconciled with the audit-table authority.

## Why "Beta" and not "RC"

`exp_20` confirms the operation algebra's **joint-A=1 conservation**
prediction at machine precision under the unitary beam splitter, but
`exp_12b` reveals that the **bare two-body system is itself
unstable** on the 6000-tick horizon at GRID=65³. That's a new open
question (where does the long-horizon two-body instability come
from?) that the paper does not yet answer. The "Beta" tag signals
a release for review with explicit open work, not a candidate for
v1.0.

## Headline changes

### exp_20 — three-arm emission operator comparison

| Arm | A_joint_drift | amp_g (final) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| A (control, drain) | 1.0×10⁰ | 1.000 | reproduces `exp_19c` row-5 non-unitarity |
| B (treatment, beam splitter) | **8.9×10⁻¹⁶** | 0.0055 | joint A=1 confirmed at machine precision |
| C (alternative, phase rotation) | 1.0×10⁻¹² | 0.000 | inert as predicted |

Arm B's photon amplitude grew to 0.236 at tick 2019 then *receded* to
0.005 by tick 4019 — a phase-dependent non-monotonic transfer not
predicted by the pure 2-mode-rotation picture, flagged as a row-6
caveat for the operation-algebra follow-on (paper #13).

None of the three arms settled the orbit; see `exp_12b` below for
why.

### exp_12b — long-duration two-body baseline (no emission)

The bare exp_12 setup run for 6000 ticks. `r_peak` escapes from R₁ to
grid edge (~83) by tick ~2000 and never returns. A=1 preserved at
machine precision throughout. Refines but does not invalidate
`exp_12`'s 4-sig-fig PASS, which scores a different (k-scan
resonance) claim. The orbital escape seen in all three arms of
`exp_20` is inherited from this baseline, not caused by the emission
operator.
