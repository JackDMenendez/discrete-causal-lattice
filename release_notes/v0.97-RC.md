## What's new in detail

### Appendix B — Induced Gauge Action

Closes the structural step of the open programme flagged in §9.7 of v0.96-RC. The Sakharov / Zeldovich claim was that the Wilson gauge action of Eq.~(9.x) should not be postulated but induced by integrating out matter — this appendix carries out the leading-order expansion symbolically:

- The bipartite plaquette holonomy on the $(\mathbf{V}_a, \mathbf{V}_b)$ parallelogram factors via Stokes as $W_{ab} = \exp(-i a^2 V_a^i V_b^j F_{ij})$.
- Summing over the three RGB plaquette planes gives a quadratic form $\mathbf{F}^T \mathbf{Q} \mathbf{F}$ with $\mathbf{Q}$ having eigenvalues $\{4, 4, 16\}$.
- After $O_h$ averaging, the leading induced action reduces to the standard Maxwell density $(\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathbf{Q}/3) \sum_{i<j} F_{ij}^2$ — modulo the numerical $1/g^2$ prefactor that requires the explicit one-loop $\mathrm{Tr}\,\ln$ calculation.
- The operator-level residual $\mathbf{Q}_\text{aniso} = \mathbf{Q} - (\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathbf{Q}/3)\mathbb{I}$ has eigenvalues $\{-4, -4, 8\}$ and aligns with the same optical axis $(1,1,-1)$ as the kinematic-sector birefringence — a closed-form gauge-sector birefringence prediction.

The full symbolic calculation is in [`src/utilities/induced_gauge_action.py`](../src/utilities/induced_gauge_action.py); captured output in [`data/induced_gauge_action.txt`](../data/induced_gauge_action.txt). Five-line SymPy reproducibility snippet included in §B.7.

## Reproducibility

The build is unchanged: `cd paper && bash build.sh` (POSIX) or `paper\build.cmd` (Windows) produces `main.pdf` from a clean checkout. All numerical claims of Appendix B are reproduced in 5 lines of SymPy:

```python
import sympy as sp
V1, V2, V3 = sp.Matrix([1,1,1]), sp.Matrix([1,-1,-1]), sp.Matrix([-1,1,-1])
F = sp.Matrix(3, 3, lambda i, j: sp.Symbol(f"F{i}{j}", real=True) * (1 if i < j else (-1 if i > j else 0)))
proj = lambda Va, Vb: sum(Va[i] * Vb[j] * F[i, j] for i in range(3) for j in range(3))
density = sum(sp.expand(proj(V, W)**2) for V, W in [(V1, V2), (V1, V3), (V2, V3)])
```

Extracting the quadratic form coefficient yields the matrix $\mathbf{Q} = ((8, 4, -4), (4, 8, -4), (-4, -4, 8))$ with eigenvalues $\{4, 4, 16\}$.
