## Why "Beta" and not "RC"?

v0.97-RC was a structural enrichment release. v0.98-RC is an
**experimental progress + reconciliation** release: two new experiments
(`exp_20` and `exp_12b`) ran for the first time, two new audit-table
rows added (both PART), the paper text was systematically aligned
with the audit-table authority via the automated consistency-check tool committed at .claude/agents/dcl-claim-auditor.md
introduced this release, and 8 follow-on/orphan notes migrated out of
dcl into a separate private repository.

The new evidence is honest: `exp_20` confirms the operation algebra's
joint-A=1 conservation prediction at machine precision, but
`exp_12b` reveals that the bare two-body system is itself unstable
on the 6000-tick horizon at GRID=65³. That opens a new question
(what is the source of the long-horizon two-body instability?) that
the paper does not yet answer. The "Beta" tag signals that this is
a release for review with explicit open work, not a candidate
for v1.0.

## What's new in detail

### exp_20 — three-arm emission operator comparison

The first experiment to test whether a unitary 2-mode rotation
(beam splitter) is the correct emission operator for the lattice's
joint A=1 conservation, and to compare it directly against the
as-written drain in `exp_19c` and the phase-rotation drain
proposed earlier as `exp_19` v5.

**Implementation:** new file `src/experiments/exp_20_emission_operator.py`,
no changes to `src/core/*` or any prior experiment file. Three arms
implemented at the experiment level using the existing
`tick(normalize=False)` API and pointwise numpy operations. Joint
A=1 enforcement implemented as `enforce_joint_unity(s1, s2, target)`,
which rescales both sessions by a common factor — preserving the
beam splitter's amplitude ratio while absorbing the bipartite tick
rule's residual drift.

**Full sweep results (2026-05-02, GRID=65³, 6000 ticks per arm,
rate=0.05):**

| Arm | result | max_streak | amp_e | amp_g | A_drift_max | rho_phi_drift_max |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| A (control) | NOT_SETTLED | 5 | 1.0000 | 1.000000 | 1.0×10⁰ | 7.6×10⁻² |
| B (treatment) | NOT_SETTLED | 4 | 0.9945 | 0.005506 | **8.9×10⁻¹⁶** | 2.5×10⁻² |
| C (alternative) | NOT_SETTLED | 8 | 1.0000 | 0.000000 | 1.0×10⁻¹² | 7.7×10⁻¹⁴ |

**Key findings:**

- **Joint A=1 confirmed.** Arm B preserves
  $\sum_i \lVert\psi_i\rVert^2$ to machine precision across the
  entire 6000 ticks. The operation algebra's central prediction is
  experimentally validated.
- **Drain non-unitarity confirmed.** Arm A reproduces the row-5
  arithmetic $(1 - 2m + 2m^2)\lVert\psi_e\rVert^2$: joint amplitude
  drift is exactly 1.0 from photon inflation at the first emission
  tick.
- **Phase-dependent non-monotonic transfer in arm B.** The photon
  amplitude grew to 0.236 at tick 2019 and *receded* to 0.005 by
  tick 4019. The cross term
  $2\,\mathrm{Re}(\sin\theta\cdot\psi_e\psi_\gamma^*)$ flips sign
  as the relative phase rotates; amplitude can pump electron→photon
  or drain photon→electron depending on phase alignment. This is a
  refinement to row 6 of the operation-algebra table that the
  follow-on paper #13 should address.
- **Orbital settling not achieved.** All three arms hit NOT_SETTLED
  with `max_streak ≤ 8`, well below the SUCCESS_STREAK = 33
  threshold. The escape behaviour is inherited from the bare
  two-body baseline; see `exp_12b`.

### exp_12b — long-duration two-body baseline (no emission)

Distinct from `exp_12` (which is a k-scan resonance-peak experiment
at PASS / 4 sig figs) and from `exp_20` (which adds emission).
`exp_12b` answers a single question: does the bare two-body system
stay bound at $R_1$ over a 6000-tick horizon at GRID=65³?

**Implementation:** new file `src/experiments/exp_12b_twobody_long_baseline.py`.
Same exp_12 init (electron + proton, mean-field Coulomb, alternating
tick order, both sessions with `tick(normalize=True)`); no photon,
no emission machinery. 6000 ticks; one worker, ~87 minutes wall
clock.

**Result:** `r_peak_min = 9.16` (close to R₁ at the start),
`r_peak_max = 83.34` (grid-edge value), `r_peak` at tick 2019 =
72.10 (already escaped), at tick 4019 = 73.85 (still escaped, no
return). `max_streak = 5` (well below SUCCESS_STREAK = 33).
A_joint_drift_max = 8.9×10⁻¹⁶ (machine precision). The orbit
escapes by tick ~2000 and never returns.

**Implications:**

- The orbital escape seen in all three arms of `exp_20` is *not*
  an emission-operator failure. It is inherited from the bare
  two-body baseline.
- `exp_12`'s PASS at 4 sig figs is *not* invalidated. `exp_12` is
  scored on a k-scan resonance peak; `exp_12b` only refines the
  temporal scope of the lock-in claim (Bohr orbits on this lattice
  are metastable, not permanently stable).
- The next investigation (`exp_12c`, deferred) should probe the
  source of the long-horizon instability: grid-size sensitivity,
  CoM-frame initial-condition residual, or accumulated kinetic-
  operator drift.

### "One law at three resolutions" — new introduction paragraph

New `\paragraph` block between the five-answers `enumerate` and the
"Two further structural results" paragraph in
`paper/sections/introduction.tex`. Three structural resolutions of
$\mathcal{A}=1$:

1. Per session: $\int |\psi_i|^2\,dx = 1$, the axiom enforced at
   every tick.
2. Joint field density:
   $\mathcal{A}_\text{joint}(x) = \sum_i |\psi_i(x)|^2$, the
   operation-algebra tag preserved across emission and annihilation
   under the unitary 2-mode rotation.
3. Clock fluid density:
   $\rho_\phi(x) = \sum_i \omega_i\,|\psi_i(x)|^2$, the
   $\omega$-weighted form whose continuity equation gives gravity.

Energy conservation is the $\omega$-weighted spatial integral.
Probability conservation, mass-energy conservation, and gravity all
share one structural origin.

This sharpens the framework's "single conservation law" claim from
assertion to identification — an additive structural result that
can be cited from the abstract's framing without further
experimental work.
