*Notes from conversation, 2026-04-02*

## The Question

How does photon emission arise from the A=1 constraint?
Is it really a conservation-of-momentum problem, or a conservation-of-probability problem?

---

## The Reframing

A Coulomb well is not a refraction.

A refraction redistributes probability *within* a session: amplitude moves from
one region to another, A=1 is preserved, the session is self-contained.

A Coulomb well is an *external source*: it injects probability amplitude at the
well centre from outside the electron session. Without a compensating mechanism,
total probability in the electron session exceeds 1. That is the A=1 violation.

The lattice resolves this the only way it can: **it opens a new session.**

---

## Photon Emission is Required, Not Emitted

The standard picture treats photon emission as a consequence of energy loss.
The A=1 picture inverts this:

- The electron locks onto the orbital attractor (phase gradient sharpens,
  PDF concentrates at R₁).
- The spatial concentration of probability requires redistribution that cannot
  be contained within the electron session alone — the Coulomb well was
  supplying amplitude from outside.
- A=1 mandates session creation at exactly this moment to receive the excess.

The photon session is not emitted as a byproduct. It is *required* as the
only A=1-consistent resolution of the orbit-locking event.

---

## Recoil Follows Automatically

The new photon session must carry the phase gradient change that accompanied
the electron's concentration. Not because momentum conservation is postulated,
but because the phase ramp that drove the concentration is exactly what gets
transferred to the new session.

The proton absorbs the remainder. The whole atom recoils — not just the nucleus
— because the phase gradient is distributed across the two-body system.

Both photon emission and atomic recoil follow from A=1 alone.

## Why exp_15 Asked the Wrong Question

exp_15 attempted to drain orbital phase artificially via a tangential phase ramp.
This fights against A=1: enforce_unity_spinor renormalises amplitude every tick,
so no net energy can be removed from a session by phase manipulation alone.

The correct mechanism is session creation, not phase drain. The photon session
IS the drain. It exists precisely to receive what A=1 cannot accommodate within
a single session when the electron locks onto its orbit.

Dissipation in the A=1 framework is not energy loss — it is session proliferation
at the moment of attractor lock-in.

## Connection to the Proton Symmetry-Breaking Story

This completes the two-body picture:

1. The live proton breaks the spherical symmetry of the Coulomb potential,
   providing the stochastic perturbation that lets the electron explore
   orbital orientation space and fall into the Arnold tongue attractor.

2. At the moment of lock-in, A=1 requires a new session (photon) to absorb
   the probability that the Coulomb well was supplying externally.

3. The photon carries the phase gradient change; the proton absorbs the recoil.

The ground state is not selected by energy minimisation. It is selected by
attractor dynamics (proton symmetry breaking) and stabilised by session
proliferation (A=1 photon creation). Energy conservation is a consequence,
not the mechanism.

## Statement for the Paper

*Photon emission is not a separate physical process imposed on the lattice —
it is the mechanism by which A=1 is maintained when a Coulomb interaction
would otherwise require probability to be created. The Coulomb potential is
not a refraction (redistribution within one session) but a source (external
injection into one session). A=1 resolves this by mandating a new session at
the moment of orbit locking. The emitted photon carries the phase gradient
change; the proton absorbs the recoil. Both follow from the single constraint,
not from separate postulates.*

## Broader Implication

This may be a general principle: **every interaction that would violate A=1
within a single session is resolved by session creation.** 

- Coulomb locking → photon session
- Pair production → two new fermion sessions
- Decay → daughter sessions

The particle zoo is not a list of things that exist. It is a catalogue of
the ways A=1 resolves probability imbalances. The Standard Model field content
may be exactly the set of session types required by the geometry of the
bipartite lattice.
