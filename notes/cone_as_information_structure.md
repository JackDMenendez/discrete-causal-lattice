## The Positive Framing

Standard physics describes the causal cone negatively — a constraint on what
cannot influence an event. This framework inverts that: the cone is an active
structure storing information about what might happen.

In the lattice this is literal. The amplitude field ψ(x,t) inside the cone at
time t contains exactly the information needed to compute all future states. The
cone stores both:
- The WHAT: amplitude magnitudes (probabilities)
- The HOW: phases (interference structure determining future evolution)

## Mass as Interior Information

This is the most precise new claim. For a CausalSession with frequency ω:

    p_stay = sin²(ω/2)   =  fraction of amplitude staying interior per tick
    p_move = cos²(ω/2)   =  fraction reaching cone boundary per tick

Mass is the fraction of the cone's information that stays inside rather than
propagating to the boundary. This is not a metaphor — it is a literal statement
about the amplitude field.

    Massless (photon):   p_stay = 0  — all information at boundary
    Massive (electron):  p_stay > 0  — information distributed through interior
    Very massive:        p_stay → 1  — information hugs the center

The energy-momentum relation E² = p²c² + m²c⁴ is then a statement about how
information is partitioned inside the cone:
- Boundary information → momentum (p)
- Interior information → mass (m)
- Total information → energy (E)

NOTE: The exact derivation from p_move/p_stay to E²=p²+m² requires working out
the dispersion relation of the tick operator in the continuum limit. The intuition
is correct; the algebra needs to be done carefully.

## What the Cone Stores

1. **Transition probabilities**: The amplitude at each interior point gives the
   probability of every possible future interaction — overlap integrals between
   this cone and future cones give interaction cross-sections.

2. **Phase coherence**: Phases encode the interference structure. A partially
   phase-scrambled cone (decoherence) has lost some of this — future paths no
   longer interfere as sharply.

3. **Conservation law information**: Topological winding numbers in the phase
   field encode conserved quantities. These are global properties of the cone,
   robust against local perturbations.

4. **Entanglement information**: After two cones interact (TickScheduler phase
   mixing), each cone stores relational information about the other. Entanglement
   is correlated cone structure.

## The Black Hole Connection

A black hole is a region where p_stay → 1 everywhere — all information forced
into the interior, none reaching the boundary. The causal cone collapses inward.

Hawking radiation: slow quantum leak of information from interior to boundary —
the cone slowly recovering its outward structure through tunneling.

Bekenstein-Hawking S = A/4: entropy proportional to surface area, not volume.
This is naturally predicted: information in the cone interior is volume-scaling,
but information observable from outside (at the boundary) is area-scaling. A
black hole's observable information is entirely at its boundary.

NOTE: This connection is suggestive but not yet rigorous. The lattice would need
to produce area-scaling of boundary information explicitly. Requires working out
the lattice equivalent of the holographic principle. Strong future direction.

## A Particle is a Stable Information Structure

A particle is a self-consistent organization of amplitude and phase that persists
because its cone interference pattern is self-reinforcing.

- Stable particle: cone returns to the same interference pattern after one full
  orbital period. Quantum numbers are the invariants of this self-consistent
  structure.

- Unstable particle: cone interference pattern not perfectly self-consistent.
  Small perturbations cause reorganization into lower-energy structure.
  Decay lifetime = how nearly self-consistent the original cone was.

This is Wheeler's "It from Bit" completed: particles are not things that carry
information — they ARE information structures. The cone is the information.
The particle is the pattern.
