**The idea:** Plasma is not primarily a thermal phenomenon. It is the phase
of matter in which the clock density gradient across the orbital radius R₁
exceeds the Arnold tongue width, making resonance-stable bound states
geometrically impossible.

**Key equation:** |∇ρ_clock| · R₁ / ρ_clock > Δω_tongue → orbit unstable

**Implications:**
- Third ionization channel beyond thermal and pressure ionization
- Minimum stable orbit radius near compact objects (atomic ISCO)
- Gradient ionization dominant near neutron star surfaces, accretion disks
- Predicts lower ionization temperature near strong gravitational gradients
  than thermal models alone
- May affect timing and structure of stellar plasma formation
- Proxy test available now: exp_strength_sweep with varying STRENGTH

paper/sections/clock_fluid_dynamics.tex §8.8

## 2. Recombination as a Clock Fluid Phase Transition

**The idea:** The cosmological recombination epoch (z ≈ 1100) is the moment
when the universe-wide clock density dropped to the point where gradients
became shallow enough for Arnold tongue resonances to form. Recombination
is not primarily a thermal event — it is the clock fluid crossing a phase
boundary.

**Implications:**
- Predicts a specific relationship between the Hubble parameter at
  recombination and the Arnold tongue width of hydrogen
- The CMB surface of last scattering is the clock fluid phase boundary
- May give a parameter-free prediction of the recombination redshift
- Dark ages end when clock fluid gradients permit the first stable orbits

**Connection to paper:** Follows directly from §8.8 gradient ionization +
§8.6 GR continuum limit (ρ_clock(t) gives the Hubble parameter)

different Arnold tongue attractor; the mass ratios are ratios of resonance

**Implications:**
- Parameter-free prediction of mass ratios (not just hydrogen)

- Predicts which masses are possible (stable tongues) and which are not

problem section), notes/harmonics_music_and_existence.md
