# Solving the EM Derivation Blocker: Induced Gauge Action

*Drafted 2026-04-25 in response to the gap identified in `em_derivation_attempt.md`.*

## The Problem
In `em_derivation_attempt.md`, Section 5 identifies a critical asymmetry:
- **Gravity's field equation** ($\nabla^2\phi = 4\pi G \rho$) emerges directly from the clock-density equilibrium (the `p_stay` bias in the tick rule).
- **Electromagnetism's field equation** (Maxwell's $\partial_\nu F^{\nu\mu} = J^\mu$) requires *postulating* the Wilson gauge action $S_\text{gauge} \propto \sum (1 - \text{Re}\,W_{\mu\nu})$. While the matter coupling ($J^\mu$) is derived from the bipartite tick rule via Peierls substitution, the dynamics of $A_\mu$ itself appears added by hand.

## The Solution: Induced Action (Sakharov / Zeldovich)
The gap vanishes when we realize that **we do not need to postulate a separate gauge action**. The dynamics of $A_\mu$ are strictly *induced* by the vacuum's existing tick rule through the requirement of $A=1$ conservation over all histories. 

This is the lattice manifestation of Sakharov's "induced gravity/gauge theory": the gauge field dynamics are generated by the vacuum loop expansions of the matter fields. 

### 1. The Vacuum as a Sea of Histories
In the discrete causal lattice, a session's propagation is a sum over histories (paths), each weighted by the tick-rule probabilities and phases.
The vacuum is not empty; $A=1$ applies globally at every tick, which means the vacuum transitions can be treated as a path integral over closed matter loops (virtual sessions popping out and annihilating).

The exact transition amplitude for the vacuum in a background $A_\mu$ (represented by link variables $U_\mu$) is formally the determinant of the lattice Dirac operator derived in Section 6.4:
$$ Z[U] = \text{det}(D_\text{lat}[U]) $$

The effective action for the background link variables is entirely generated by the matter paths:
$$ S_\text{eff}[U] = -\text{Tr} \ln(D_\text{lat}[U]) $$

### 2. The Plaquette Term is Structurally Forced
When we expand the trace $\text{Tr} \ln(D_\text{lat}[U])$ as a sum over closed loops on the lattice, each hop along a link contributes a factor of $U_\mu$. 

Because of **gauge covariance** (derived in `em_derivation_attempt.md` Sec 2) and **locality**, the effective action can only depend on gauge-invariant closed loops. 
The leading-order, smallest, most dominant non-trivial loop on the discrete causal lattice is the **plaquette** $W_{\mu\nu}$. 

By the symmetries of the bipartite $\text{T}_{diamond}^3$ geometry (which is isotropic at the leading order of the lattice spacing $a$), the expansion of the induced action is heavily dominated by the single-plaquette trace:
$$ S_\text{eff}[U] \propto \sum_\text{plaquettes} \left( 1 - \text{Re}\,W_{\mu\nu} \right) + \mathcal{O}(\text{larger loops}) $$

### 3. Recovering Maxwell
As already established in `em_derivation_attempt.md` Sec 3, the plaquette expands in the continuum limit to the field strength tensor:
$$ 1 - \text{Re}\,W_{\mu\nu} \;\xrightarrow{a \to 0}\; \frac{a^4}{2} F_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu} $$

Therefore, the induced effective action *is* the Maxwell action:
$$ S_\text{eff}[A] \propto \int d^4x \, F_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu} $$

Setting the variation to zero ($\delta S_\text{eff} = 0$) simply enforces that the vacuum is stable (a state of maximal transition probability / strictly satisfying $A=1$ globally when integrated over dynamic histories), which directly yields:
$$ \partial_\nu F^{\nu\mu} = J^\mu $$

## Conclusion
We **do not** need to postulate the Wilson action. 

1. The bipartite tick rule + Peierls substitution forces the matter to couple to $A_\mu$ locally.
2. The $A=1$ constraint means all possible path histories must unitarily sum to 1.
3. The closed-loop vacuum paths around the lattice (trace log of the hopping operator) induce an effective energy for the twist $A_\mu$.
4. Locality and the lattice geometry restrict the leading term of this induced energy to exactly the Wilson plaquette action.
5. The Wilson plaquette action yields Maxwell's equations.

**The asymmetry is resolved:** Gravity falls out of the classical statistical equilibrium of clock density (the divergence/scalar channel), while EM falls out of the quantum/path-integral vacuum polarization of the phase twist (the curl/tensor channel). Both are pure consequences of the exact same lattice hopping rules, without a single postulated field equation.
