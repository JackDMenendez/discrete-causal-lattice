## The Claim

Standard physics treats three conservation laws as independent:
- Conservation of energy
- Conservation of momentum
- Conservation of probability (unitarity)

## What A=1 Actually Is

A=1 is not "unitarity" in the quantum mechanical sense. It is simpler:

> At every tick, the total probability across all nodes of a session equals 1.

This is conservation of probability in its most primitive form. No Hilbert space,
no Hermitian operators, no Noether theorem. Just: probability is not created
or destroyed.

From this single statement:

**Energy conservation** — A closed set of sessions that exchange phase gradients
must balance. The total phase gradient across all sessions is conserved because
A=1 forbids net probability creation in any session. What we call energy is the
time-integrated phase mismatch; its conservation is a consequence of A=1 applied
to closed session sets.

**Momentum conservation** — Phase gradient balance across sessions. When two
sessions interact, the phase ramps must sum to zero (equal and opposite) or
a third session must be created to absorb the remainder. Conservation of momentum
is A=1 applied to phase gradients across space.

**Photon emission** — The Coulomb well is a probability source (not a
refraction). It injects amplitude into the electron session from outside.
A=1 cannot accommodate this in a single session; it mandates a new session
(photon) at orbit lock-in. The photon carries the phase gradient change.
The recoil is absorbed by the proton session. Both follow from A=1 alone.
