\caption{%
  \textbf{Double-slit interference on the $\Tdiamond$ lattice (the Huygens
  Lantern).}
  Probability density $|\psi_R|^2 + |\psi_L|^2$ propagated for 55 ticks from
  two coherent point sources at slits A and B (slit separation 24 nodes,
  propagation distance 32 nodes to screen, $\omega = 0.15$).
  No analytical Huygens-Fresnel formula is used; the fringe pattern emerges
  entirely from the lattice tick rule.
  %
  \textit{Left}: coherent illumination. Two amber wavefronts propagate
  diagonally along the RGB/CMY basis directions from slits A and B and
  overlap at the screen ($x = 40$, dashed vertical line). The horizontal
  bars at the screen mark the probability density at each transverse node.
  %
  \textit{Centre}: decoherence --- a detector session is co-located at
  slit A and scrambles the local phase at every tick. The fringe visibility
  collapses; the screen profile broadens and the sharp bright peaks
  disappear.
  %
  \textit{Right}: 1D screen intensity profiles. The warm (orange) curve is
  the coherent case; glowing white dots mark the bright fringe peaks ---
  the Huygens lanterns. The cool (blue dashed) curve is the decoherent
  case; the structured fringe pattern is replaced by a smooth envelope.
  The two slit positions A and B are marked with dotted horizontal lines.
  %
  The fringe contrast (Michelson visibility $\mathcal{V} > 0.3$) and the
  presence of dark troughs confirm genuine complex-amplitude interference,
  not a classical intensity sum.
  Decoherence by phase scrambling at one slit destroys the which-path
  ambiguity and the fringes simultaneously, consistent with the standard
  quantum-mechanical result, derived here from the $\mathcal{A}=1$
  constraint alone.
}
\label{fig:lanterns}
