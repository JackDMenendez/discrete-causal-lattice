\noindent\textbf{What changed in v0.96-RC (since v0.95-RC).}
A correctness pass on the frame-condition derivation, prompted by a
SymPy verification, revised the central isotropy claim of the paper.
The previous text asserted
$\sum_\text{RGB}\mathbf{v}_i\mathbf{v}_j^T = 3\delta_{ij}$;
direct calculation shows the actual frame matrix is
$\begin{pmatrix} 3 & -1 & 1 \\ -1 & 3 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$
with eigenvalues $\{4, 4, 1\}$.  The corrected derivation recovers
$\mathrm{SO}(3)$ rotational invariance only on $O_h$-averaged
observables; the residual operator-level anisotropy is a
\emph{lattice birefringence} with closed-form coefficients and an
optical axis along $\mathbf{V}_1+\mathbf{V}_2+\mathbf{V}_3 = (1,1,-1)$.
This is a sharp, falsifiable prediction strengthening the framework's
falsifiability profile, not a weakening of its claims.

The affected sections are: the abstract bullet (i), the conclusion's
first structural claim, the isotropy paragraph in
Section~\ref{sec:kinematics}, the 1/$r^2$ derivation in
Section~\ref{sec:gravity}, and the photon-dispersion prediction P7
in Section~\ref{subsec:p7_photon_dispersion} (which now includes a
direction-resolved refinement).  Section~\ref{sec:kinematics} also
gains a new "what drives the oscillation" paragraph identifying
two competing hypotheses for the source of the Zitterbewegung rate
$\omega$, distinguishable in principle by direction-resolved
precision-mass measurements.

A more detailed change log lives in \texttt{release\_notes/v0.96-RC.md}
in the project repository.  Earlier versions remain available at the
Zenodo deposit's prior DOIs.
