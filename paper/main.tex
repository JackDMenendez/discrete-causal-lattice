\noindent\textbf{What changed in v0.97-RC (since v0.96-RC).}
v0.97-RC is a structural enrichment release.  Where v0.96-RC corrected
a wrong claim and surfaced the lattice-birefringence prediction,
v0.97-RC builds on that foundation with three sets of additions:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{New Appendix~\ref{sec:induced_gauge_action} (Induced
    Gauge Action).}  Symbolic derivation of the structural form of
    $-\mathrm{Tr}\,\ln D_\text{lat}[U]$ on the bipartite octahedral
    lattice.  The leading term has the Maxwell form on $O_h$-averaged
    observables, with an operator-level residual ---
    \emph{gauge-sector birefringence} --- aligning along the same
    optical axis $(1,1,-1)$ as the kinematic-sector prediction.  This
    closes the structural step of the §9.7 open program; only the
    numerical $1/g^2$ prefactor remains for a follow-on paper.
\item \textbf{New P9 (Multi-Channel Concordance,
    Section~\ref{subsec:p9_concordance}).}  Elevates the framework's
    strongest empirical claim into its own prediction: the kinematic,
    gauge, gravitational, and (under the recoil-driven Zitterbewegung
    hypothesis) inertial-mass anisotropies all share a single optical
    axis.  Five independent observation channels (GRB time-of-flight,
    CMB polarisation rotation, LIGO/Virgo polarisation-mode timing,
    atomic-clock comparisons, anomalous-dispersion anisotropy) are
    predicted to agree.  Inter-channel disagreement falsifies the
    inheritance-from-bipartite-geometry claim.  Three time-dependence
    scenarios (uniform, domain, dynamical embedding) are
    distinguishable by sidereal modulation and redshift-resolved
    binning.
\item \textbf{Three sets of paragraph adds tightening the
    substrate-level unification.}  §7.2/§9.1/§9.6: the topological
    potential and the gauge field are both \emph{virtual-session
    clouds}, distinguished only by the sublattice signature of the
    source's internal flow.  §13.3/§15.7: the Arnold tongues are the
    lattice realisation of the classical Lorentz oscillator's
    resonance set.  §6/§14/Conclusion: Lie-theoretic restatement of
    the lattice as a discrete subgroup of $SE(3)$ carrying $SU(2)$
    representations, with the central conjecture sharpened from
    ``open question'' to ``precise finite-dimensional Lie-algebra
    calculation.''
\item \textbf{Plato closing question} in the Conclusion: the
    projection-vs-film question is rendered empirical via P9's
    time-dependence sub-prediction.
\item \textbf{New Figure~\ref{fig:induced_action_ellipsoid}}: dual
    rendering of the kinematic frame matrix $M$ (prolate ellipsoid in
    $\mathbf{k}$-space) and the gauge plaquette tensor $\mathbf{Q}$
    (oblate ellipsoid in $\mathbf{B}$-space) sharing the same optical
    axis $(1,1,-1)$.
\end{itemize}

A correctness sweep also eliminated four residual sites that v0.96-RC
missed (in introduction, conclusion, P3, and phase\_propagation), and
added a canonical \texttt{eq:optical\_axis} label cross-referenced
across the paper.  The audit table was updated with five new rows
(kinematic birefringence, gauge-sector birefringence, induced gauge
action, Zitterbewegung-source dual hypothesis, P9 concordance) plus
\texttt{exp\_16}/\texttt{exp\_19c} status reword.

A more detailed change log lives in \texttt{release\_notes/v0.97-RC.md}
in the project repository.  Earlier versions remain available at the
Zenodo deposit's prior DOIs.
